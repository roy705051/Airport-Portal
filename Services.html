1. Flight Bookings: Airlines allow customers to book flights online, through their websites or mobile apps. They provide a range of options for selecting departure and arrival cities, dates, and times.

2. Baggage Handling: Airlines handle baggage services, including checking in luggage, tagging it for the correct destination, and transporting it to the aircraft. They also provide guidelines on baggage size, weight limits, and restrictions.

3. In-flight Services: Once onboard, airlines offer various in-flight services to passengers. These can include complimentary meals, snacks, and beverages, as well as entertainment systems with movies, TV shows, music, and games. Some airlines also provide Wi-Fi connectivity on certain flights.

4. Seat Selection: Passengers can usually select their seats during the booking process, choosing from different options such as window, aisle, or extra legroom seats. Some airlines may charge an additional fee for preferred seat selection.

5. Special Assistance: Airlines offer special assistance for passengers with disabilities, medical conditions, or specific needs. This can include wheelchair assistance, priority boarding, and accommodation for service animals. Passengers requiring special assistance can usually request it during the booking process or by contacting the airline in advance.

6. Loyalty Programs: Many airlines have loyalty programs, such as frequent flyer programs, that reward regular travelers with benefits and privileges. These programs often offer upgrades, priority check-in, access to airport lounges, and the ability to earn and redeem points for future flights.

7. Flight Status and Notifications: Airlines provide information on flight schedules, delays, cancellations, and gate changes. Passengers can receive updates through email, SMS, or the airline's mobile app.

8. Customer Service: Airlines have customer service representatives available to assist passengers with inquiries, changes to bookings, refunds, and other issues. Support may be provided through phone, email, social media, or in-person at the airport.
